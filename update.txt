	Here's an honest assessment:

	What's solid

	The stack is well-chosen and coherent. Next.js 15 with static export + Vercel is a proven, low-maintenance combo. Firebase for a simple form backend is appropriate — it avoids over-engineering while leaving room to grow. next-intl is the right choice for App Router i18n, and React Hook Form + Zod is standard. Nothing exotic, nothing that will bite you later.

	The brand definition is unusually clear for a V1 spec — having hex codes, font families, and mood already locked down saves real time.

	The out-of-scope discipline is the strongest part of this document. Many agency projects die because V1 keeps expanding. You've drawn the line firmly.

	Real concerns

	RTL is harder than it looks. Trilingual EN/FR/AR with RTL isn't just adding a dir="rtl" attribute. Tailwind has RTL utilities (rtl: prefix), but every layout, every flex direction, every icon, every card needs to be verified. Budget extra time specifically for this — it's commonly underestimated.

	Firebase Functions for email notifications is a cold-start liability. For a low-traffic site that gets one inquiry every few hours, a Firebase Function may be cold and slow. Consider a simpler alternative like Resend or Nodemailer via a lightweight API route — or even just Firestore + a CRON-based email digest. Not a blocker, just worth flagging.

	5 pages with per-tour and per-hotel inquiry forms means form duplication. If each tour card has its own inquiry form and there are many tours, you'll want a single shared modal/drawer with the tour name passed as context — otherwise you're maintaining N forms. The spec doesn't address this pattern.

	Content dependency is the real risk. The "Content Needed From ENTEG" section lists everything correctly, but Algerian SMEs are notorious for slow content delivery (this isn't a cultural judgment — it's true of small businesses everywhere). If ENTEG doesn't deliver photos, the site ships with placeholders. Consider building the component structure to accept placeholder content gracefully, and set a hard deadline in the contract.

	No mention of SEO structure. For international tourists finding you via Google, metadata, Open Graph tags, and hreflang for three languages are important. Next.js makes this easy with the Metadata API, but it's not in the spec at all.

	Google Analytics 4 may be blocked. A portion of users — especially international ones — use ad blockers. Not critical, but worth noting. A privacy-friendly fallback like Plausible (which works better in some regions) could be an option.

	Minor gaps

	No mention of a sitemap or robots.txt (relevant for international SEO)
	No error/404 page
	No loading/skeleton states for the tour and hotel grids
	WhatsApp link strategy needs a decision: floating button on all pages, or only on Contact? Both is common for Algerian sites
	Overall verdict

	This is a solid, realistic V1 spec. The scope is honest, the stack is appropriate, and the brand is well-defined. The main execution risks are RTL complexity and content delivery delay — neither is an architectural problem, both are project management problems. If you lock down content delivery terms upfront and budget proper RTL QA time, this is a buildable, shippable project.
